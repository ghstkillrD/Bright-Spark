Our Mission

Our mission is to revolutionize early childhood education by leveraging advanced technology to enhance preschool education and support children with learning disabilities. We aim to provide timely, personalized interventions that enhance cognitive, emotional, and social development through engaging, developmentally appropriate educational games. Our ultimate goal is to empower parents and educators with tools that facilitate continuous monitoring and collaboration, ensuring every child has the opportunity to reach their full potential.

Domain

Our Research Domain

Our research domain focuses on leveraging technology to enhance early childhood education and development, particularly for children aged 3-5. In response to the increasing integration of electronic devices into daily life and the critical role of e-learning, our study addresses the early identification of learning disabilities such as ASD, ADHD, Dyslexia, and DCD. "Bright Spark" aims to monitor and improve children's academic and behavioral progress. This application not only streamlines data collection for educators but also facilitates parental involvement through enhanced communication and collaboration.
To enhance educational outcomes, "Bright Spark" incorporates carefully curated game-based learning interventions. These games are designed to stimulate cognitive development, enhance motor skills, and promote social interaction among children. This system aims to make learning enjoyable and This commitment allows parents and educators to confidently utilize Bright Spark as a trusted application for enhancing early childhood education, fostering holistic development, and addressing learning challenges proactively.

Capturing and Recording Progress of a Child

Bright Spark facilitates child development by integrating standard teaching curriculums and assessment methods from both local and global contexts. This innovative approach allows educators to continuously assess and update each child's progress based on predefined criteria. Bright Spark enables timely interventions and personalized support strategies to optimize learning outcomes for children ages 3-5.

Key Objectives:

  • Parents can track their child activities in real-time.
  • Both teachers and parents can see student development monthly.
  • Teachers can access student reports and time from anywhere.

Identify Learning Disability Symptoms

Bright Spark is dedicated to identifying common learning disorders especially ASD, ADHD, Dyslexia, and DCD among preschool-age children. By analyzing recorded child data and generating progress reports using those data, the app provides parents with valuable insights into their child's development. This proactive approach helps in the early detection of potential challenges, enabling parents to take informed actions and collaborate effectively with educators. Through clear and accessible progress reports, Bright Spark supports parents in understanding and supporting their child's unique learning journey, fostering a supportive environment for optimal growth and development.

Key Objectives:

  • Genernnte monthly psychological progress reports of children.
  • Derive the child's current status based on the progress reports generated.
  • Generate monthly comparisons to verify and clarify the child's progress.

Addressing Learning Disability Symptoms Through Games

By leveraging the popularity and effectiveness of game-based learning, this feature aims to address early symptoms associated with disabilities such as ASD, ADHD, Dyslexia, and DCD. Through carefully selected games that target specific cognitive, motor, and social skills, children can engage in enjoyable activities under the supervision of parents or teachers, while their progress is monitored and assessed. This approach not only enhances learning but also provides valuable insights to parents and educators regarding a child's developmental milestones and any areas that may require additional support.

Key Objectives:

  • Recommends appropriate games to address identified learning disability symptoms.
  • Engage children in enjoyable activities under supervision.
  • Monitor and assess progress through game-based learning.

Optimizing UX Design for Preschool Age Children, Parents and Teachers

Focuses on a user-centered design, considering children's cognitive, emotional, and physical development, to create visually appealing and accessible designs that ensure a positive experience for preschoolers, parents, and teachers.

Key Objectives:

  • User-centered, engaging interface for preschool-age children and parents.
  • Incorporate positive emotional responses and motor skill-based interactions, ensuring usability for preschoolers and parents alike.
  • Address usability challenges and provide a balanced user experience.
